FAQ

What is a Moon?
A solid object in orbit around a planet, dwarf planet, minor planet, or transneptunian object is called a "satellite." A natural satellite is sometimes referred to as a "moon" in popular usage.

What is a Black Hole?
A region of space having a gravitational field so intense that no matter or radiation can escape.

How many planets are there in our solar system?
Our solar system officially has eight planets and one star: the Sun. In order from the Sun out, the planets are: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Is Pluto really a planet?
No. The official arbiter of such questions is the IAU and they have decided that Pluto should be classified as a dwarf planet, a category distinct from regular planets.

Do all objects in the universe exert force on all other objects?
All objects in the universe exert a gravitational force on all others. But, the farther away an object is, the smaller is the force it exerts.

Which planets have rings?
The four gas giants, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune, have rings.

What are globular star clusters?
A globular star cluster is a collection of up to a million stars that all share the same origin.

What is a galaxy?
A galaxy is an enormous collection of gas, dust and billions of stars held together by gravity.

What is escape velocity?
Escape velocity is the speed a moving object must have in order to escape from the gravitational pull exerted by another object.
